INTRODUCTION

Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan, stands as a meticulously planned metropolis that marries political significance with natural beauty. Beyond its political role, Islamabad offers a vibrant cultural scene with events, art exhibitions, and a diverse population that reflects Pakistan's rich mosaic of cultures. Its educational institutions, including renowned universities and research centers, have earned it a reputation as an academic center. Additionally, Islamabad's modern infrastructure, cleanliness, and an emerging tech industry are contributing to its development. The city's iconic Faisal Mosque, Pakistan Monument, and Rawal Lake are just a few of the attractions that make Islamabad a unique blend of urban sophistication and natural splendor.
